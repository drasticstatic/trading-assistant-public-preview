ES is the **institutional barometer** of the index trio.
When smart money wants to defend a position, ES is often
where they do it first — because the S&P includes the
defensive sectors that act as a floor during selloffs.

**Why this matters:**
If NQ makes a new low but ES holds above its key level,
that's the divergence signal. The institution is defending
the S&P while letting the Nasdaq (more momentum-driven)
flush out retail shorts. The setup then becomes a potential
LONG on ES, using the NQ divergence as confirmation.
