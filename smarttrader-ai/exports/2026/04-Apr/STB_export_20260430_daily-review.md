## 🧠 Behavioral Notes

### The Wider Session Picture

This session is best understood as a multi-instrument monitoring session where one position happened to fill, not as a single-instrument trade day. Christopher was watching YM, RTY, NQ, ES, MCL, and MGC simultaneously with resting orders placed at conservative levels across all of them. The intent was proportionate — expecting larger moves before fills, not anticipating an aggressive pre-market sweep of the YM level.

This is part of a broader pattern worth naming clearly: **the live session plan and the formal premarket plan are two different documents.** The formal premarket plan rarely survives contact with the open unchanged — levels shift, scenarios evolve, the overall structural bias holds but the specific instruments and entries change in real time. This is not necessarily a failure of planning. Many experienced traders operate exactly this way. The gap is not in the plan's flexibility — it is in the protective layer that must travel with every live position regardless of how the entry was identified. A resting limit placed the night before needs a resting stop placed at the same time, or the account floor becomes the stop by default.

### The Momentum vs. Reversal Calibration

Christopher has named something important: he has spent his trading career anticipating reversals while struggling to take momentum trades. The YM short at 49278 was a reversal thesis in a session that turned into a momentum session to the upside. RTY running +0.99% while NQ barely moves is a momentum signal in Dow and small-caps — not a short-Dow reversal context.

This is not a flaw that ends trading careers. It is one of the most common calibration issues among technically-skilled traders. The structural read was not wrong — YM *did* have levels and a broader downtrend context. What was missing was the real-time filter: when the open session character contradicts the pre-placed thesis, the thesis gets re-evaluated before the position absorbs more damage. The SMT divergence data was available live. The coach named it. The position held anyway.

The calibration work here is specific: **when live SMT divergence inverts against an open position, that is an exit signal, not a hold signal.** Not a maybe, not a "let's see if it resolves" — an exit instruction. Building that reflex is the next layer of the work.

## 🔑 Key Lessons

1. **Resting limits require resting stops.** Any limit order left active unattended — overnight, pre-market, or during a break — must have a corresponding stop order placed at the same time. There is no version of a resting limit without a stop that is safe on a prop firm account.

2. **SMT divergence against an open position is an exit signal.** When the session opens and the index character inverts against the thesis (YM leading while NQ flat = not a short-YM day), the position gets reassessed immediately, not held to resolution.

3. **The premarket plan is a structural scaffold, not a script.** The bias and key levels from pre-market planning are durable. The specific entries are not. When the live session diverges from the planned scenario, updating the entry plan is not abandoning the plan — it is trading the plan correctly.

4. **APEX-06 protection is the job.** TPT reset-3 is gone. The profit target on APEX-06 is still alive. Every decision from here is filtered through one question: does this protect or endanger the APEX account?
