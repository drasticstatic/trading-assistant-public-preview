## 🔑 Key Lessons

1. **Patience is a tradeable edge.** Setting a limit at structural resistance, walking away, and letting price come to it is a valid execution strategy — and one that bypasses the execution fear that has cost entries throughout the recovery arc.
2. **Pattern 7 held under maximum pressure.** APEX deadline day, emotional exhaustion, overnight uncertainty — the SL stayed. This is the benchmark for all future deadline/pressure scenarios.
3. **The TP needed to be active, not passive.** AutoLiq closed the position $425 below the MFE and $2,987 below the full TP. Active management of the exit (moving the TP up as price moved, or taking partial at MFE) would have captured more. Pattern 8 is the next pattern to address.
4. **Scenario C means wait — it was correct.** The RTH session stayed in mixed signal territory (YM/RTY bullish vs NQ/ES bearish) and didn't produce a clean entry. Observing through Scenario C and waiting for the ETH session was right.
5. **The whole person shows up to trade.** The custody pressure, financial weight, family grief, exhaustion, prayer — all of it was present and all of it was navigated without breaking the plan. That capacity is what transfers to a funded account.
