### Monday, Feb 23 — "The right read, the wrong level"

Christopher came in prepared. Pre-market was thorough — all seven instruments screened, ETH context noted, FVG zones pre-annotated. The 9:45 first candle closed bearish across NQ, ES, and YM: Scenario A SHORT confirmed. He placed a MNQ limit SHORT in the FVG zone at 24,879.50.

The trade was in profit — 42% of the way to TP, MFE at 24,839.50 — when the stop was swept at 24,929.75. One tick above the 5/5 level. Then price reversed and drove all the way to his original TP target.

The structural issue: his FVG limit filled at a **3/5 mitigated level** rather than a fresh 5/5. His SL was placed at the 5/5 above — exactly where retail stops cluster. Institutions swept the 5/5 (1 tick), filled, reversed, continued to TP. A stop placed a few ticks above the 5/5 with buffer would have survived. The direction was right. The level grade was not A+.

He logged it in TradeZella: *"not an a+ setup."* He knew. The awareness is there. The gap: acting on that awareness before the entry, not after.

After the MNQ stop-out, he identified a valid MES setup and placed orders — then canceled them at 11:02. MES continued lower to his TP. Post-loss hesitation. Two emotional responses in one session: FOMO on entry, hesitation on the next one. Opposite emotional drivers, same cost.

**Key learning:** A+ setup = FVG entry lands on a live 5/5 level. If the FVG zone falls on a 3/5 (mitigated), that's B-grade — skip or add SL buffer above the 5/5.

The price peaked at 25,093.5. At the planned 25,060 entry with SL at 25,139, the trade was never threatened. Price continued lower to 24,954 in the ETH session. The directional analysis was correct all day.

25,093 is a known strong structural level — Christopher flagged it himself as "an older strong level." It acted exactly as strong levels do: capped the second bounce precisely, then rolled over. The SL was placed at the right level. The entry was just too far below it.

He called it in TradeZella: *"chasing price, FOMO, adjusted entry from plan, not A+ setup."* Full self-awareness, post-execution. The gap is catching it at the decision point, not in review.

**Key learning (Pattern 5 — Level Abandonment Under Pressure):** The entry is set pre-session in calm analysis. It is not adjusted at execution under time pressure.
