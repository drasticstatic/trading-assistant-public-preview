## 🔍 Behavioral Patterns — What the Data Shows

### 🔴 Pattern 1 — Stop Movement (80%+ of significant losses)

Every single account-ending trade has "kept moving stoploss," "moved tp & sl," or "bleed" documented in TradeZella. This one behavior is responsible for the majority of the -$9,095 loss. Examples:

- Apex Reset-1 (Feb 13): Original stop estimate ~$200–$400. Actual loss with stop movement: -$3,080. **Stop movement cost: ~$2,680 in a single trade.**
- Apex Static (Feb 9): Fixed $625 drawdown — any stop movement eliminated the entire buffer immediately.

Without stop movement, this period has a completely different outcome. The setups were good. The entries were often right. The exits were negotiated — and that's the entire problem.

### 🔴 Pattern 2 — Post-Win Overconfidence (The Dopamine Trap)

| Date | Setup that worked | Immediate next action | Damage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feb 12 | CL +$1,080 (best trade of the period) | New ES position — emotional, not A+ | -$3,000, account blown |
| Feb 13 | ES +$1,075 | New ES position (same minute, same direction) | -$2,000, account blown |

The large win doesn't signal "I'm in the zone." It signals the dopamine circuit is activated and safeguards are about to be overridden. The mandatory 15-minute break rule after any win exists precisely because of this pattern.

### 🔴 Pattern 3 — Same-Day Account Resets (Funding the Spiral)

On Feb 13, two accounts were reset and blown within 45 minutes of each other. Opening a fresh account while emotionally compromised does not reset the emotional state — it funds a second, third, and fourth attempt at the same bad trade. New rule: **no account resets on the same calendar day as a blowup. Minimum 24-hour wait.**

### 🟡 Pattern 4 — Pre-Session Bias Not Established (Paper Trading)

17 paper trades, 2 wins (11.8% win rate). "Ignored bias" tagged on 6+ trades — trading long in confirmed downtrends and vice versa. The 1H directional map is not being built before entries. This is a mandatory pre-session step going forward, not optional.
