## 🧠 7. Behavioral Notes

This trade should not have been taken. Christopher knows this — he said so directly before asking for the reviews.

The driver: frustration about the YM result and specifically about the exit passivity. The ungratefulness feeling he described — not at the market, but at himself for hesitating when the moment was there — is exactly the state that leads to revenge entries. The market had closed a painful chapter and the instinct was to quickly recoup some of the sting before the session ended. The 44-second hold is almost a physical expression of that impulse: get in, get out, feel like I did something.

It was profitable. That makes this more dangerous than a loss would have been. A loss here would have been a clean lesson. A small win reinforces the behaviour — it tells the nervous system that last-minute revenge entries sometimes work. They don't, systematically — but the single data point of +$20 will make this pattern easier to repeat.

**Exit efficiency of 9.52%** on a winning trade is the lowest possible signal. MFE was $28; collected $20. In 44 seconds, Pattern 8 still operated.

**No stop** on a short in a strong uptrend at market close, two contracts, with 44 seconds before the session ends. The risk management here is entirely dependent on market timing luck.

The right response to the YM frustration was not another trade. It was what Christopher did afterward: walked the dog, came back, updated the journal. That was the correct sequence. The MCL entry happened in the gap between YM closing and that recovery — next time, the journal opens before the order panel.
