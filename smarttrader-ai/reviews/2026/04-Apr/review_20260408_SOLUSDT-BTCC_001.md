**Pattern 8 — Exit Passivity (recurring):**

This is the fourth consecutive trade where the exit was passive (SL hit, AutoLiq, or resting TP) rather than active and deliberate. The skill gap here is *acting on the exit thought when it arrives*, not reading the trade.

**Pattern 9/10 echo — Deadline Pressure Entry:**
The voucher expiration created artificial urgency. The trade had to happen by 11:30 PM regardless of optimal signal timing. This is the same pressure dynamic as the eval compliance trades — external deadline overriding signal purity. The direction was right, the setup was reasonable, but the entry was voucher-motivated.

**New observation — Pre-Entry Directional Whiplash:**
At the moment of entry, Christopher almost reversed from SHORT to LONG. He held the SHORT. The read was ultimately incorrect (SOL reversed up), but this isn't clear evidence the LONG would have been better — it was indecision at execution, not a superior read. Worth tracking: how often does the last-second doubt appear, and is it signal or noise?

**What this is NOT:**
This is not evidence of inability to read the market. Christopher's 20/20 hindsight and frustration at "knowing where it was going" is exactly right — he often sees the structure clearly. The gap is in the micro-level: when to commit, and when to exit once committed. These are trainable, mechanical skills. Not character flaws.

## 🎯 Forward Focus

1. **Exit rule — practice the thought becoming action:** When the exit thought arrives, treat it as a signal, not a suggestion. Paper trade or BTCC: if you think "should I get out here?" — get out. Build the reflex before size matters.
2. **Voucher trades are reps, not evaluations:** The loss stings because of the week's weight, not because of its dollar value. Keep the context clear — these are practice swings, not the game.
3. **Pre-entry indecision:** If the last-second doubt is strong enough to consider flipping the direction, that is a signal to *wait*, not to flip. A confident entry should feel like conviction, not a coin flip at 11:29 PM.
