## 🧠 3. Behavioral Notes

This trade has two separate problems that deserve to be understood independently.

**Problem 1: The entry was structurally unsound.**
The bias was downtrend. YM went long. That's not a setup — it's a hope. Entering at the bottom of a large displacement candle into a confirmed downtrend is the same pattern that appeared on Apr 23 RTY and was flagged then. It has now happened again. The phrase "ignored recent bias in order to stick to my plan" shows self-awareness but also reveals the real issue: the plan should have been re-evaluated before entry when it conflicted with bias. A plan that runs directly against the day's confirmed direction is not a plan worth sticking to.

**Problem 2: Pattern 8 dominated the exit.**
The MFE was +$670. Christopher saw the manipulation wick. He was ready to pull the trigger. He did not. From MFE (+$670) to final exit (−$1,205), the cost of that one decision was $1,875 in unrealized potential. This is not about regret — it's about pattern recognition. Pattern 8 has now manifested in exactly this way — seeing the profit, recognizing the moment, not acting — multiple times. It will keep manifesting until the mechanical rule is explicit: *when the pre-planned TP level is hit, the order executes, full stop.*

**What was good:** Christopher did not move his stop (no stop to move). He exited manually before the prop firm auto-close at 17:00. He journaled the data honestly. These are real credits.

**A note on the emotional aftermath:** Feeling sick about this trade despite a green day is a known cognitive trap — the mind fixates on the missed +$670 rather than anchoring to the actual outcome (+$355 net day). The YM loss was real, but it did not break the account, it did not violate the minimum balance, and the day is green. That matters.
