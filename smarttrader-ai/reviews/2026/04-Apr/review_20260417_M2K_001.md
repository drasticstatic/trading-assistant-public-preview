Exit was via clock (approaching 5 PM hard stop territory), not a decision. Zero active exits in recent memory — exits continue to occur via SL, resting TP, AutoLiq, or time. This is the defining pattern of this period.

**TP and SL both canceled at fill:**

"Trade Risk: 0.0" in TradeZella confirms this. It is unclear whether TradingView canceled them automatically (bracket order configuration issue) or they were manually canceled immediately after fill. Either way, the effect is the same: no protective orders for 8+ hours.

**Counter-trend bias:**
TradeZella notes market context as "bullish on higher tf, uptrend." The trade sought a reversal. The ZTH coach noted this week that Christopher tends to look for reversals rather than continuations — this trade is a direct example. The market was in an uptrend, the short was a counter-trend reversal trade, and the trend continued.

**FOMO at entry:**
TradeZella lists "fear of missing out" as part of the entry logic alongside "ob, key levels." This suggests the level may have been reached or the price had already moved and the limit was placed in a reactive manner rather than planned well in advance of price arrival.

**MES canceled at 11:52 AM:**
A SELL MES (correlated instrument) was entered and immediately canceled 3 hours into the trade. This may reflect a moment of reconsidering adding a hedge or confirmation trade — but the decision was ultimately to do nothing with it. Monitors but doesn't act is a recurring theme.

**One positive:**

> *"I notice I look for reversals more so than continuations and hearing Jesse say that the market is typically trending. If I made that simple change or did everything opposite what I do, I've caught both continuations and reversals cleanly before, but recently I have been getting an incorrect feel again often."*

Christopher is self-aware about the reversal bias — this is the same insight that came up in the ZTH 1-on-1 debrief. The practical application is: before entering any counter-trend trade, the five-layer filter should include a deliberate "is the trend broken or just pausing?" check. A pause in an uptrend is not a reversal.

On exits: Christopher had a profitable MFE on this trade within 8 minutes of the fill and still held for 8 more hours. Establishing one non-negotiable exit rule (e.g., "if MFE reaches X points and pulls back 50%, exit immediately") would address this at a structural level rather than relying on real-time judgment.
