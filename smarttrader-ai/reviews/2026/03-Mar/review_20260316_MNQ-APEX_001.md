**SL adjustment during trade:** Christopher moved the SL during the live trade, rationalizing it as alignment with the FCR long-from-here ray. This is a new variant of the SL movement pattern — not the same as Pattern 7 (panic removal), but still an in-trade SL change tied to a reason that emerged *after* entry. The original SL placement was presumably based on pre-entry analysis. Once in a trade, changing the SL to a new structural level resets the original risk calculation. The result: stopped out when the adjusted level broke, even though price had already moved meaningfully toward TP.

**Frustration noted:** Christopher flagged frustration with the stop movement. That self-awareness is healthy — it means the pattern is visible to him.

**Pattern 7 context:** The original Pattern 7 fix (stepping away from the screen under pressure) applies when the impulse is to *remove* the SL entirely or move it far. This variant — moving the SL to a "rational" structural level during a live trade — deserves its own awareness. Not coaching this unless Christopher asks.
