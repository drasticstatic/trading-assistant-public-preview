## ⚠️ The Core Issue — 3/5 Level + SL at 5/5

This is the only thing that needs to change.

### What happened structurally:

```
Chart level stack at time of trade:

5/5 level:  ~24,929  ← SL was placed HERE
                           ↑ (49 pts above entry)
3/5 level:  ~24,879  ← ENTRY was HERE (FVG fill)
                           ↓ (96 pts below entry)
TP target:  ~24,783
```

The **3/5 level** is a *mitigated* 1hr color flip —
it has already been tested. It carries less institutional
weight than a fresh 5/5.

The **5/5 level** at ~24,929 is the REAL structural
reference. In ZTH methodology, the 5/5 is where
institutional activity is strongest. It is also where
**retail stop orders cluster** — traders naturally place
stops at or just above the nearest 5/5. Institutions
know this. The sweep of the 5/5 by exactly 1 tick before
reversal is not a coincidence. It is the playbook.

### The specific failure mode:

```
Enter at 3/5 (weaker level)
→ SL placed AT the 5/5 above (natural structural stop)
→ The 5/5 is where stops are clustered
→ Institution sweeps the 5/5 to collect liquidity
→ Your stop gets filled (1 tick above 5/5)
→ Institution reverses and drives price to TP
→ You got stopped out of a correct trade
```

This pattern — **stop hunt at the 5/5 then resume** —
is one of the most common mechanisms in liquid futures.
The 5/5 level is a liquidity magnet precisely because
it is a known institutional reference.

## 💡 The Adjustment — Two Options Going Forward

### Option A: 5/5 Entries Only (Recommended)

```
FCR A+ Level Quality Rule:
→ The FVG limit entry MUST align with an active 5/5 level
→ If the FVG zone lands on a 3/5 (mitigated):
   → Grade = B setup
   → Either SKIP or reduce to minimum size
→ If the FVG zone lands on a 5/5 (active):
   → Grade = A+ setup
   → Full FCR execution per the E-Book
```

A 3/5 entry with a 5/5 stop creates a gap that is
exploitable. A 5/5 entry with a stop ABOVE the 5/5
(with buffer) gives the level room to breathe.

### Option B: 3/5 Entry — Add SL Buffer

```
If entering at a 3/5 and SL is placed at the 5/5:
→ Add 3–5 ticks buffer ABOVE the 5/5
→ The stop should be at: 5/5 level + 3 ticks
→ Not AT the 5/5 (where stops cluster)
→ This clears the sweep zone

In this trade:
→ 5/5 SL level: 24,928.75
→ Adjusted SL with buffer: 24,930.50–24,932.00
→ Stop fill was at 24,929.75
→ A 3-tick buffer would have kept the trade alive
→ Trade would have hit full TP (+$192 instead of -$100)
```

### The Combined Rule:

```
BEFORE ENTERING:
1. What grade is this FVG entry?
   → Aligns with 5/5: A+ → proceed with full size
   → Aligns with 3/5: B  → skip OR min size with buffer

2. Where is my SL?
   → AT the 5/5: vulnerable to sweep → add 3-5 tick buffer
   → ABOVE the 5/5 (with buffer): protected ✅

3. Is this an A+ setup?
   EMA + 5/5 level + trend + SMT confirmation = A+
   Missing any one condition = B grade or lower
```

### The rule:

```
After a loss:

→ Evaluate the next setup on its OWN merits
→ If it's an A+ setup: take it
→ If you're hesitating because of the loss: don't take it
```
