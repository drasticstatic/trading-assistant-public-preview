Key Trading Principles Discussed
Prop Firm Trading Strategy
Consistency valued over large single-trade gains - $10,000 over two weeks
preferred to $10,000 in one trade
First take profit recommended for accounts with consistency rules
TCL 2.0 trades finish quickly (often 3 minutes), ideal for prop firms
Range Importance
Ranges below 1.5% on crypto become extremely volatile and dangerous
Minimum 0.2% range recommended for futures (equivalent to 2% on crypto)
Small ranges can stop out traders even with 200 EMA protection
Timing Considerations
Avoid trading within 30 minutes of New York open (before 10:00 AM)
Trading window preference: 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM
Volatility concerns around market open times

SMOG Setup Reviews
Fib Level Placement for SMOG
Place fib from high to low at bottom of retracement after trend break
Target entry at 50 or 618 fib levels (the "golden pocket")
Entries too low (236) or too high (786) have lower win probability
Stop loss typically placed outside 786 level
Example 1: Poor Setup
Strong consensus: Bad setup (mostly 1s and 2s)
Critical issues: ADX above 25 on both 1-min and 5-min (34 and 33), no change of
character, small fair value gap, taking long into bearish fair value gap
Additional concern: No clear targets due to nearby bearish fair value gaps
Key lesson: Even winning trades can be bad setups - this relied solely on 618
bounce to work
Example 2: Solana - Excellent Setup
Strong agreement: Good setup (mostly 4s and 5s)
Strengths: Previous day high sweep from London, decisive trend break with
actionable fair value gap between 50-618, ADX at 25, multiple confirmations
Entry strategy: When fair value gap midpoint is between 50-618, target the
midpoint; prefer entries closer to 618 for better risk factor
Stop loss placed above 786 to cover wicks
Outcome: Break of structure achieved, 4R target hit

Important Technical Concepts
200 EMA Placement
Ideal: 200 EMA at or near entry level for TCL setups
High 200 EMA increases volatility risk
Change of Character
Must be decisive, not small wicks
Similar to trend breaks - need clear, strong movement
Fair Value Gaps
Larger gaps are better - small gaps often get disrespected
Prefer gaps that encompass both 50 and 618 fib levels
Taking positions into opposite fair value gaps is dangerous
ADX Analysis
Not just about being below 25 - context matters
Watch whether ADX was climbing during previous trend, then declining
ADX declining after uptrend signals potential shorts; ADX declining after
downtrend signals potential longs
Trading Psychology & Risk Management
Pulling Trades Early
Valid to close at breakeven or partial loss if setup deteriorates
If limit 1 is missed and higher high/higher low pattern forms, consider exiting
Personal preference: Prioritize avoiding losses over catching every winner

Journaling Mistakes
Use TradesLog "mistakes" section to track early exits
Write detailed notes about reasoning - identifies fear vs. legitimate technical
concerns
Helps determine if overrisking or still processing previous losses
Pair Selection & Focus
Spend time with 1-2 pairs maximum to build relationship with price action
Each pair moves differently (Ethereum vs. AVAX vs. XRP)
More screen time with specific pairs = better fib placement accuracy
Quality Over Quantity
Avoid forcing 4-5 TCL setups per day - can lead to burnout and losses
Taking a loss after multiple wins feels like wasted time - better to wait for quality
setups
