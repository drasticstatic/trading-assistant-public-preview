Key Smog Strategy Confluences
Fair Value Gaps
Act as built-in intraday support/resistance levels
Create "springboard effect" when price retraces to fill liquidity
Bullish FVG: avoid shorts when price approaches
Bearish FVG: avoid longs when price approaches

Daily Bias Indicators
Determines trading direction for first 60-90 minutes of session

Smog Trade Setup Rules
Entry Requirements
Target Fibonacci retracement levels between 50% and 61.8%
Avoid entries at 23.6% level (too shallow)
Place stop loss outside 78.6% Fibonacci level for breathing room 
Minimum risk-to-reward at break of structure: 1.3R to 1.4R (prefer 2R)  
Fair Value Gap Placement
Prefer stop loss outside of fair value gap zones
If choosing between 50% and 61.8%, prioritize healthier range over exact
Fibonacci level
Stop loss placement more important than precise entry level
Trade Management
Take partial profits at break of structure
Move stop loss to breakeven after taking partials 
Trail stop loss on 5-minute Heiken Ashi candles 
Alternative: watch for reactions at 15-minute fair value gaps 
Confluence Stacking Philosophy
Why Stack Confluences
Base strategy win rates range from 30-50%
More confluences = higher win rate
Being hyper-selective (3-5 trades per week) with high confluence led to fewer
back-to-back losses 
Better to have fewer high-conviction trades than many low-conviction ones

TCL Strategy Essentials
Core Requirements
Trending price action (not choppy/consolidating)
All EMAs aligned in direction of trend
20 and 50 EMA near entry level
Clean, consistent price action (avoid large parabolic moves) 
200 EMA Confluence
Primary confluence for TCL setups
Minimum: 200 EMA should be above Limit 2
Ideal: 200 EMA aligned with Limit 1 (1.0) or Entry Level (2.0)
When aligned with entry, price typically bounces for Limit 1 win
If price breaks below, often retraces back to 200 EMA for Limit 2 win 
Range Management
Prefer healthier range over larger range with better EMA alignment

Trade Management Best Practices
ADX Interpretation
ADX climbing above 40-50 indicates strong trend
High ADX = poor environment for reversal strategies (Smog)
High ADX = good environment for trend strategies (TCL/G2)
Use ADX to determine which strategy suits current market conditions 
Pair Selection
Limit watchlist to 1-2 pairs for consistency
Larger pairs (SOL, ETH, LTC) have deeper order books and cleaner price
action 
Smaller pairs (MANA, ATOM) have more volatile wicks and higher slippage risk
Build a "relationship" with your trading pairs to understand their behavior 
Bitcoin Correlation
Most altcoins correlate with Bitcoin movements
For major pairs, can do timeframe analysis on the pair itself
For smaller altcoins, consider doing macro analysis on Bitcoin

Q&A Highlights
On Fair Value Gaps
Recently produced bullish FVG will springboard up toward older bearish FVG
Focus on the FVG that will cause the rejection, not the one that will draw price
1-minute FVGs have less weight unless aligned with Fibonacci levels or other
confluences  
On Opposite Signals
Normal to see opposite Smog signals during retracement on entries
Signals trigger automatically when ADX below 25
Don't let opposite signals invalidate setup if other confluences align 
On Consolidation
Brief consolidation before retracement doesn't invalidate trade
Extended consolidation (10-15 minutes) makes past TA less relevant
If consolidation causes entry level adjustment, consider waiting for better
setup
On Reversals During Trends
Identify daily bias for first hour of session
Stick to directional bias even if opposite signals appear
Use change of character, underside retests, and Fibonacci levels to confirm
reversals
Follow 1-minute rules strictly to avoid counter-trend losses
