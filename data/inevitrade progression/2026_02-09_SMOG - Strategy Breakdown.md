Entry Criteria Overview
ADX indicators: Need one or both ADX indicators at 25 or below to signal
potential reversal   
Below 25 indicates weakening trend and high probability of reversal
One-minute ADX for micro level, five-minute for macro level
OG oscillator: Produces buy/sell symbols when ADX hits 25 or below  
Can use on-chart version or oscillator view
Works hand-in-hand with ADX indicators
Change of character (CHoCH): Required to validate reversal pattern  
Uptrend example: higher highs/lows → lower high → lower low
confirms CHoCH
Often produces fair value gap at 50-61.8% Fibonacci levels 
Fair value gaps (FVG): Key entry zones when aligned with Fibonacci levels
Target 50% or 61.8% levels for entries (the "SMOG zone") 
Place stop loss outside 78.6% level for breathing room 
15-Minute Confluences for Directional Bias
Session sweeps: Check for sweeps of previous session highs/lows
(London, Asia, New York)  
Strong bearish sentiment when previous high is swept and rejected
Can lead to massive trades (up to 29R mentioned) 
OG oscillator highlights: Look for signals within 2-hour window before
trading session

1-Minute Confluences for Entry
Underside retest (most important): Draw trendline and wait for break
before entering   
Entry at trendline break + fair value gap = ideal reversal point
Saves many trades by avoiding premature entries 
Fibonacci level alignment: FVG should be at 50% or 61.8% levels  
Lower levels (23.6%, 38.2%) are less reliable
78.6% used primarily for stop loss placement  
Multiple fair value gaps: Prefer larger FVGs at better Fibonacci levels over
smaller ones at wrong levels   
Trade Setup and Risk Management
Entry points: Target midpoint of FVG or 50%/61.8% Fibonacci levels 
Stop loss placement: Outside 78.6% level minimum, provides breathing
room without killing R-factor    
Minimum 1.5R from entry to break of structure is healthy range  
Fibonacci direction: High to low for shorts, low to high for longs   
Trade Management Methods
Fixed take profit: Simple 4R target for full position 
Partial profit method (recommended):
Take 50% (or 25% minimum) at break of structure

Trailing stop methods:
Fair value gap method: Trail stop to nearest major FVG 
More subjective, requires skill at identifying FVGs
Heiken Ashi method (recommended for most traders):   
Use 5-minute Heiken Ashi candles for smoother trailing
Set timer for 4:30 and adjust stop to high of closed candle
Less mental bandwidth required   
Systematized approach works better for beginners  
Chart Layout and Tools
Dual timeframe setup: 1-minute on left, 15-minute on right  
Use sync feature to align price levels across charts
Helps avoid taking trades into opposite directional FVGs   
Indicators needed: ICT Kill Zones & Pivots, IT SMOG/G2, OG Oscillator 
Settings shared: Chart layout, oscillator settings, and Fibonacci settings

Trading Philosophy and Expectations
Conservative approach: 2-3 high-conviction trades per week preferred
over 10-15 low-conviction trades  
Win rate focus: Stack confluences to increase winning percentage over
chasing large R-factors   
Without 15-minute bias: Expect 2-5R trades with lower win rate (~50-
60%)   
With full confluences (SMOG 3.0): Can achieve higher win rates and larger
moves
