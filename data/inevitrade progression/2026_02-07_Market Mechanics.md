Market Structure and Participants
Market hierarchy explained - Four levels determine price movement: 
Market makers at the top level manipulate markets to fill institutional orders
and earn fees
Institutional players are the biggest reason for market manipulation,
especially during session opens 
Algorithms control price during low participation periods (e.g., crypto
weekends) 
Retail traders have minimal impact on price movement, even large retail
accounts  
Order Types and Leverage
Primary order types - Buy and sell limits are used for most strategies 
Buy/sell stops mainly used for partial profits on futures positions 
Leverage considerations for crypto:
Higher leverage increases liquidation risk significantly 
Risk of complete account loss if stop loss doesn't fill before liquidation
level 
Important to maintain buffer between stop loss and liquidation price 
Futures leverage structure:
Mini contracts = 10 micro contracts
Micro contracts (MES, MNQ, MGC) preferred for accounts under $100K  
Allows comfortable risk management proportional to account size

Evaluation funnel explained:
Large pool of applicants pay evaluation fees ($50-$200)
Most traders fail evaluation phase and pay for resets 
Small percentage pass to verification phase 
Many blow accounts in verification despite passing evaluation 
Only handful reach funded status where real payouts occur 
Revenue model - Prop firms profit primarily from failed evaluation attempts
and resets, not from trader losses  
Payout structure - First $10K typically paid in full, then 90/10 split after
threshold  
Evaluation timeline - 30-day window to pass, subscription-based with
automatic renewal  
Crypto Trading Considerations
Exchange selection factors:
Maker and taker fees vary significantly between exchanges
Leverage limits differ by platform
Order book depth critical for larger accounts 
Mexi preferred for high leverage and low fees, but KYC is now required 
Manipulation and liquidity issues:
Smaller altcoin pairs experience heavy stop hunting 
Market makers chase clustered stop losses to collect fees 
Larger pairs (Solana, Etherium, LTC, AVAX) have deeper books and less
manipulation  
Fee avoidance strategies:
Use post-only option for limit orders to take maker fees instead of taker
fees
Maker fees significantly lower than taker fees

Crypto vs Futures Trading
Crypto Pros:
24/7 trading availability with no market breaks
Low barrier to entry (can start with $100)
High leverage access without massive capital requirements
No rules or restrictions on personal capital
Flexible scheduling
Crypto Cons:
Higher manipulation, especially during session opens
More volatile and less regulated
Retail failure rate extremely high
Futures Pros:
Highly regulated and professional environment
US-based platforms easier for US residents
No hidden fees or payout concerns
Clean KYC process with standard identification
Futures Cons:
Limited trading hours with maintenance breaks (4-6PM EST)
Restricted timeframes for optimal trading
Requires prop firms or ~$50K minimum for direct trading 
Prop Firm Rules and Strategy
Psychological benefits of rules - Despite being restrictive, prop firm rules
build good trading habits  
Force consistent smaller wins over high-risk trades
