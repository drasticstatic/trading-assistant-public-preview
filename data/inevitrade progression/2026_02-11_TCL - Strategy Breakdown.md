What is TCL?
TCL (Trend Continuation Level) is a multiple order dip buy level trend
continuation strategy 
Evolved from TC1s (single limit order targeting 1:4 risk-reward) to increase
trade frequency and win rate  
Two versions available: 1.0 (step-up calculator with 3x and 5x multipliers)
and 2.0 (even split 1:1) 
Version 1.0 breakeven rate is approximately 83-85%, target 90%+ win rate
in backtesting before going live  
Version 2.0 offers approximately 0.44:1 risk-reward vs 0.22:1 for 1.0, with
faster rebound from losses (3 trades vs 5-8 trades)

Version 1.0 Setup and Execution
Fibonacci levels: Entry, two dip buy levels (Limit 1 and Limit 2), stop loss at

Uses step-up calculator with different quantities for each level (1x, 3x, 5x
multiplier)   
Must manually verify and input correct quantity for each dip buy level  
Average risk-reward per trade: 0.22-0.23:1 across all three levels  
If entry take profit hits, cancel remaining limit orders  
If limit one fills, adjust take profit quantity to "all" to combine positions     
Version 2.0 Setup and Execution
Fibonacci levels: Entry at lower level, only one dip buy level (Limit 1), stop
loss at 0  
Uses even split calculator with same quantity for both orders (1:1 ratio)   
Average risk-reward approximately doubles to 0.44:1 per trade  
Faster recovery from losses (3 trades vs 5-8 for 1.0)  
Entry take profit at 61.8% Fibonacci level 
Recommended for prop firm trading due to better consistency and
drawdown management  
Extended Take Profit Method
Optional: Use extended TP to target 1.272 level (full 1:1 risk-reward) instead
of standard 61.8%  
Take 25-50% partial profits at 61.8%, move stop loss to breakeven, cancel
remaining limit orders    
Creates risk-free trade with potential for 1R total profit  
Historical data shows approximately 60% of trades retrace to breakeven
rather than hitting extended TP 
Not recommended for prop firms as they reward consistency over home
runs

Key Confluences for Trade Selection
Simplicity Model: Primary confluence method recommended for beginners
200 EMA placement: Should be around the 382 Fibonacci level (near Limit
1)    
Ideal EMA structure: 20 and 50 EMAs near 61.8% level, 200 EMA near 382
level, all evenly spaced  
If 200 EMA too close to stop loss, setup is risky; if slightly below entry is
acceptable    
Additional confluences available: VWAP, extreme support/resistance,
dealing range (add after mastering basics)  
Trading Guidelines and Best Practices
Only trade one TCL at a time, especially when starting out    
Never take two simultaneous TCL positions as it doubles risk and margin
usage   
Avoid trading Bitcoin directly due to highest volatility; Solana and Ethereum
are preferred pairs  
Cancel trade if EMAs consolidate and flip direction during position   
If you miss entry and price already hit TP, wait for new lower low/higher
high before entering   
For Femex users: stick to larger pairs (Solana, Ethereum) due to order book
depth limitations

Choosing Between 1.0 and 2.0
Use 1.0 if backtesting shows 40%+ of wins are entry take profit wins  
Use 2.0 if majority of wins are limit one or limit two (typical for most
traders)   
2.0 recommended for prop firm accounts due to better drawdown
management and consistency  
Personal trading style matters: 1.0 offers larger single wins, 2.0 offers more
consistent smaller wins
