### Core Psychological Concepts
**Market Neutrality**
- Markets don't reward confidence, intelligence, or social skills 
that work in regular life
- The market is presenting neutral information; traders attribute 
emotions to data
- Being right or wrong is irrelevant - the market only rewards 
probabilistic edge over time
- Conventional success traits often work against traders
**Managing Expectations**
- Happiness = Expectation minus Reality
- Traders must enter each trade fully accepting uncertainty with 
zero expectations
- Expecting specific outcomes creates emotional attachment and pain 
when trades don't work out
- The goal is to execute edge over time, not predict individual 
trade outcomes
**Confirmation Bias & Self-Deception**
- The mind actively filters information to protect from emotional 
pain
- During backtesting, traders often favor setups they want to work, 
skewing results
- Denial and rationalization prevent traders from seeing reality in 
their data
- Every trade must be explainable - if you can't explain why you 
took it, the data is corrupted
**Subconscious Beliefs vs. Conscious Logic**
- Awareness alone isn't enough - belief overrides logic every time
- Example: knowing a snake is safe doesn't eliminate fear; only 
repetition rewires the subconscious
- Traders must override limiting beliefs through high-repetition 
practice in low-risk environments
### The Glass Equity Ceiling
**Key Concept**
Craig introduced the "glass equity ceiling" - a psychological 
barrier where traders repeatedly hit the same account peak before 
suffering drawdowns.  This isn't random or market-driven; it's a 
subconscious belief creating a financial ceiling.  
**Why It Happens**
- Traders change behavior when approaching their ceiling (taking 
profits early, reducing risk)
- The behavior that got them to the ceiling (consistent execution) 
gets abandoned
- This pattern is clearly visible in equity curve data
**How to Identify**
- Look for repeated peaks at the same account level in equity curves
- Examine trades taken at these levels to identify behavioral 
changes
- Grant (a successful Inner Circle graduate) struggled with this 
same issue
### Framework for Probabilistic Thinking
**Five Keys to Success**
1. Fully accept risk on every trade - if you fear the loss, 
something is wrong  
2. Treat each moment as unique - past outcomes don't determine 
future results     
3. Execute without hesitation, especially after losses  
4. Act on edge even when it feels uncomfortable - best trades often 
feel risky  
5. Remove all emotions from market data - perceive information 
objectively  
**Mental Shift Required**
Moving from psychological prisoner to probabilistic thinker means:   
- Shifting from seeking certainty to embracing uncertainty
- Accepting risk as opportunity cost to feed your system
- Perceiving all information objectively without emotional 
attachment
- Seeing every moment as unique and free from past trauma
### Live Trading & Practice
**Importance of Real Money**
- Paper trading is valuable for learning execution but limited for 
psychological development
- Even $10-$20 risk creates real trading experience that paper 
trading cannot replicate
- Paper trading for a month without real money was "the biggest 
waste of time"
- Recommended progression: replay → paper → small live capital
**Repetition Requirements**
- High repetition in safe environments rewires subconscious beliefs
- Practice should be done with small capital where losses don't 
cause emotional pain
- The goal is to identify psychological barriers in data and 
deliberately practice overcoming them
### Strategy Discussion
**Smog vs. TCL**
- Smog: more traditional, friendlier for beginners, better for 
larger starting capital
- TCL: higher win rate, better for compounding small accounts 
quickly, riskier
- G2 signals are opposite of Smog - taken when ADX shows trending 
conditions
- Both strategies complement each other - when one doesn't work, the 
other often does
**Strategy Selection Advice**
- Choose one strategy and master it before learning another
- Don't switch strategies during a losing streak - give each 
strategy a full process trial
- If prop trading, risk level matters less since you can restart
- Bootcamp will focus primarily on Smog and TCL as these have the 
best proven results
**Market Edge Evolution**
- Edges constantly change as markets evolve
- The community continuously tests and adapts strategies
- Trading market mechanics that are fundamentally consistent 
provides stability
